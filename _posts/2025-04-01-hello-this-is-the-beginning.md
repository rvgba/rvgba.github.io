Eight years into my career, I've decided to start writing. Not because I think my thoughts are particularly special, but because I've realized the act of writing forces clarity in a way that thinking alone doesn't.

This blog is going to be a bit of everything: reflections from the world of project management and product operations, observations about design and visual culture, and occasionally, an honest account of what it's like to navigate a career at the intersection of business, tech, and creativity in Jakarta.

## What I Do

I spend my days as a Special Projects Manager at Gojek — coordinating across product, engineering, operations, and policy to ship things that (ideally) make life measurably better for Indonesian MSME merchants. It's messy, fast, and rarely goes exactly to plan. I love it.

Before that I spent years at GoPay, building the KYC infrastructure, SOPs, and operational systems that underpinned one of Indonesia's most-used payment platforms at its most critical growth phase.

## Why Design Too?

I've been told it's unusual — a PMO who cares deeply about typography and grid systems. But to me the connection is obvious: both are fundamentally about communicating something clearly to someone else. A well-designed SOP and a well-designed editorial layout share the same core challenge — how do you organize information so the reader doesn't get lost?

I studied economics, not design. But I grew up in creative spaces — student publications, event branding, magazine layouts. Design has been my creative outlet in a career otherwise defined by spreadsheets and stakeholder decks.

## What to Expect Here

I'll write when I have something worth saying — probably somewhere between once a week and once a month. Topics will range across:

- **PMO & operations**: the unglamorous, essential work of building systems that scale
- **Design observations**: things I find beautiful, broken, or worth analyzing
- **Indonesia**: the context that shapes how I see everything
- **Personal**: the rest of it

Thanks for reading. Let's see where this goes.
